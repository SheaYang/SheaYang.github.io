I'm a final year Ph.D. student at the Center for Cosmology and Particle Physics CCPP of New York University. I am currently a research assistant of Dr. Anthony R. Pullen's group and a member of the Experiment for Cryogenic Large-Aperture Intensity Mapping (EXCLAIM) survey science group.

My recent researches focus in probing the galaxy evolution processes and interstellar medium properties with measurements, simulations and analytic models. I am interested in: 1. Developing data analysis method in intensity mapping signal measurement and foreground removal. 2. Creating mock line intensity mapping data with galaxy formation semi-analytic models. 3. Developing methods for fast and robust semi-analytic model calibration. 4. Interstellar model building.
